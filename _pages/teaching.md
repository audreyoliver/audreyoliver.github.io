## Teaching Overview

My teaching emphasizes the integration of mathematical theory with computational implementation. I aim to help students move beyond symbolic manipulation and develop the ability to translate mathematical concepts into reproducible, well-structured code.

In computational courses, I focus on clarity of reasoning, model interpretation, and the importance of verification and validation in numerical work. I encourage students to experiment with parameters, visualize behavior, and critically evaluate model assumptions.

## Programming in Mathematics (Math 340)

As a Teaching Assistant for an upper-division undergraduate course in mathematical programming, I led weekly laboratory sections and developed structured Jupyter notebook exercises to reinforce lecture material.

Lab topics included:

- Python fundamentals for mathematical computation  
- Numerical methods and approximation  
- Logistic maps and nonlinear dynamics  
- Cubic splines and interpolation  
- Activation functions and modeling  
- Symbolic computation and visualization  

My labs were designed to connect analytical concepts with hands-on computational experimentation, emphasizing reproducibility and clean coding practices.

## Instructional Approach

In computational instruction, I prioritize:

- Translating theory into executable models  
- Building intuition through simulation and visualization  
- Encouraging structured, readable code  
- Developing independent problem-solving skills  

I view computational literacy as an essential component of modern mathematical and scientific training.
